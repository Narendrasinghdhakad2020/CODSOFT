Tim Berners-Lee

< The man who invented World Wide Web.>

Former physicist Tim Berners-Lee invented the World-Wide Web as an essential tool for High Energy Physics (HEP) at CERN from 1989 to 1994. Tim is now Director of the Web Consortium W3C, the International Web standards body based at INRIA, MIT and Keio University.

Timeline of Tim Berners-Lee's Life:

  • 1955 - born in London, England, UK.

  • 1969 - He attended Sheen Mount Primary School, and then went on to attend south west London's Emanuel School.

  • 1973 - went to The Queens College, Oxford,where he received a first-class bachelor of arts degree in physics. While at university, Berners-Lee made a computer out of an old television set, which he bought from a repair shop.

  • 1978 - He joined D. G. Nash in Ferndown, Dorset, where he helped create type-setting software for printers.

  • 1980 - Berners-Lee worked as an independent contractor at CERN from June to December 1980. While in Geneva, he proposed a project based on the concept of hypertext, to facilitate sharing and updating information among researchers.[28] To demonstrate it, he built a prototype system named ENQUIRE.After leaving CERN in late 1980, he went to work at John Poole's Image Computer Systems, Ltd, in Bournemouth, Dorset.

  • 1984 - The project he worked on was a "real-time remote procedure call" which gave him experience in computer networking. In 1984, he returned to CERN as a fellow.

  • 1989 - CERN was the largest internet node in Europe, and Berners-Lee saw an opportunity to join hypertext with the internet.

  • 1991 - The first website was built at CERN. Despite this being an international organisation hosted by Switzerland, the office that Berners-Lee used was just across the border in France. The website was put online on 6 August 1991 for the first time.

  • 1994 - Berners-Lee founded the W3C at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.

  • 2001 - He became a patron of the East Dorset Heritage Trust, having previously lived in Colehill in Wimborne, East Dorset.

  • 2004 - He accepted a chair in computer science at the School of Electronics and Computer Science, University of Southampton, Hampshire, to work on the Semantic Web.

  • 2009 - Berners-Lee admitted that the initial pair of slashes ("//") in a web address were "unnecessary". He told the newspaper that he easily could have designed web addresses without the slashes. "There you go, it seemed like a good idea at the time", he said in his lighthearted apology.

  • 2013 - The Alliance for Affordable Internet (A4AI) was launched and Berners-Lee is leading the coalition of public and private organisations that includes Google, Facebook, Intel, and Microsoft.

  • 2016 - he joined the Department of Computer Science at Oxford University as a professorial research fellow and as a fellow of Christ Church, one of the Oxford colleges.

  • 2018 - Berners-Lee announced a new application made by open-source startup Inrupt based on the Solid standards, which aims to give users more control over their personal data and lets users choose where the data goes, who's allowed to see certain elements and which apps are allowed to see that data.
